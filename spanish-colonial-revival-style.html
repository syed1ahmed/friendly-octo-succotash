How to Bring Spanish Colonial Revival Style into Your Home: A Step-by-Step Guide

Spanish Colonial Revival architecture draws its inspiration from the grandeur of Spanish colonial buildings in the Americas.This style is all about creating warm, inviting spaces that celebrate the romance of a bygone era.

Juli Smith
July 2023

Drawing from my own experience as an architect, I've had the privilege to work with Spanish Colonial Revival architecture, a captivating style that emerged in the United States during the early 20th century. This architectural gem takes inspiration from the grandeur of Spanish colonial buildings in the Americas. It beautifully weaves together Spanish, Moorish, and Mediterranean influences, showcasing red-tiled roofs, stucco exteriors, wrought-iron details, and ornate arches.

It's no wonder this style is often associated with a sense of romanticism and nostalgia, as it effortlessly transports us to a bygone era. Moreover, Spanish Colonial Revival architecture has made an enduring mark on the American landscape, especially in regions with a rich Spanish heritage like California, Florida, and the American Southwest, where its unique charm and cultural significance continue to be celebrated."

Key Elements of Spanish Colonial Revival Interior Design

1. Stucco Walls

The foundation of Spanish Colonial Revival interiors often features stucco walls. The texture and earthy tones of stucco create a sense of rustic authenticity.

2. Arched Doorways and Windows

Arches are a hallmark of this style. Incorporate arched doorways and windows to add a touch of drama and sophistication to your spaces.

3. Terracotta Tiles

Flooring plays a pivotal role in Spanish Colonial Revival interiors. Opt for terracotta tiles with their warm hues and distinctive patterns. These tiles bring a touch of the Mediterranean to your home.

4. Wrought Iron Details

Ornate wrought iron railings, light fixtures, and accents add a touch of elegance and nostalgia. They can be both functional and decorative, making them a versatile choice.

5. Wooden Beams

Wooden ceiling beams, often exposed and left in their natural state, add a rustic element that complements the overall aesthetic.

6. Rich, Earthy Colors

Embrace a palette of warm, earthy colors like terracotta, deep reds, and rich browns, reminiscent of the Spanish countryside.

7. Talavera Tiles

For a pop of vibrant color, consider incorporating Talavera tiles in your kitchen or bathroom. Their hand-painted designs bring life to any space.

Creating Your Spanish Colonial Revival Oasis

Now that we've covered the key elements, it's time to put your design ideas into action:

1. Furniture

Opt for wooden furniture with intricate carving and wrought iron accents. Upholster sofas and chairs in rich, textured fabrics like leather or suede for a touch of luxury.

2. Accessories

Decorate with Spanish-inspired accessories such as ceramic pottery, vibrant textiles, and traditional wall art.

3. Lighting

Choose wrought iron or wrought iron-look chandeliers and sconces to cast a warm, inviting glow throughout your home.

4. Outdoor Spaces

Don't forget your outdoor areas. Create a Spanish Colonial Revival courtyard or patio with a central fountain, terracotta planters, and lush greenery.

5. Personal Touches

Add your personal style to the mix. Incorporate family heirlooms or travel finds that resonate with the Spanish Colonial Revival spirit.

Conclusion

Spanish Colonial Revival architecture is a design style that effortlessly blends the old and the new, creating spaces that are both elegant and comfortable. Whether you're renovating an entire home or simply looking to infuse a touch of Old World charm into your living room, this timeless style offers a wealth of inspiration. So, embrace the warmth, embrace the history, and embark on a journey to create your own Spanish Colonial Revival oasis right in the heart of your home.
